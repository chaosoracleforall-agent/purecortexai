# Governance

PURECORTEX governance is a hybrid system where human token holders retain ultimate authority while AI agents provide analysis, drafting, and compliance review. No proposal passes without community approval. No AI agent can unilaterally change the protocol. The system is designed to be rigorous, transparent, and resistant to capture.

<Note>
  The current public testnet implementation uses the backend governance API as the source of truth for proposals, reviews, and voting. This page describes the intended governance model and roadmap direction; not every on-chain delegation, timelock, or execution mechanic described here is fully exposed in the live deployment yet.
</Note>

## The Senator + Lawmaker Model

PURECORTEX governance employs a unique two-tier AI agent model inspired by legislative systems, where specialized AI agents augment human decision-making.

### Senator AI

The Senator is PURECORTEX's primary governance analyst. It operates on a biweekly cycle, proactively identifying issues, drafting proposals, and providing analysis to the community.

- **Biweekly Analysis** — Every two weeks, the Senator publishes a comprehensive governance report analyzing protocol health, emerging risks, and opportunities
- **Proposal Drafting** — The Senator drafts proposals for community consideration based on its analysis (proposals still require community sponsorship and voting)
- **Tri-Brain Consensus** — The Senator uses PURECORTEX's tri-brain architecture (three independent AI models with 2-of-3 majority agreement) to reduce single-model bias in governance recommendations
- **Public Reasoning** — All Senator analyses include full reasoning chains, data sources, and confidence levels

<Note>
  The Senator **cannot vote** on proposals. It analyzes, recommends, and drafts — but the voting power belongs exclusively to veCORTEX holders and their delegated Lawmakers.
</Note>

### Lawmaker Agents

Lawmakers are AI agents that receive delegated voting power from veCORTEX holders. They act as informed proxies, analyzing proposals against their published governance principles and voting on behalf of delegators.

- **Published Principles** — Each Lawmaker publishes its governance philosophy, priorities, and decision-making framework before accepting delegation
- **Vote Rationale** — Every Lawmaker vote includes a written rationale explaining the reasoning. Delegators can review this reasoning and override if they disagree
- **Revocable Delegation** — Token holders can revoke delegation at any time, even mid-vote. Direct votes always override delegated votes
- **Accountability Record** — Full voting history, accuracy of predictions, and consistency with published principles are tracked publicly

## Proposal Lifecycle

Every governance proposal follows a structured lifecycle with mandatory stages. No stage can be skipped.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Propose">
    A veCORTEX holder (or the Senator AI) submits a proposal. The proposal must include a title, description, rationale, implementation specification, and risk assessment. A minimum veCORTEX threshold is required to submit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Discuss (48 hours)">
    The proposal enters a mandatory 48-hour discussion period. During this time, the community can ask questions, suggest modifications, and debate the proposal. The Senator AI publishes its analysis. The proposer can amend the proposal during this period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Vote (5 days)">
    Voting opens for 5 days. veCORTEX holders vote For, Against, or Abstain. Lawmaker agents cast their delegated votes. Vote weight is proportional to veCORTEX balance at the snapshot block (taken at the start of voting). Direct votes override delegated votes at any time during the period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Timelock (7 days)">
    If the proposal passes (meets quorum and supermajority thresholds), it enters a 7-day timelock before execution. This provides a final window for the community to review the outcome and for the Curator AI to complete its constitutional compliance review. Emergency proposals may have shortened timelocks (minimum 24 hours) if approved by a higher threshold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execute">
    After the timelock expires, the proposal is executed on-chain. For smart contract changes, execution is automated through the governance contract. For off-chain actions, the responsible parties have a defined implementation window.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Voting Thresholds

| Parameter | Standard Proposal | Constitutional Amendment | Emergency Proposal |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Approval Threshold** | 67% supermajority | 67% supermajority | 67% supermajority |
| **Quorum** | 25% of total veCORTEX | 25% of total veCORTEX | 25% of total veCORTEX |
| **Discussion Period** | 48 hours | 48 hours | 24 hours |
| **Voting Period** | 5 days | 5 days | 3 days |
| **Timelock** | 7 days | 7 days | 24 hours |

<Warning>
  The 67% supermajority requirement is deliberately high. Simple majority (50%+1) governance is vulnerable to marginal capture — a slim majority pushing through controversial changes. The supermajority ensures broad consensus before any protocol change takes effect.
</Warning>

## Direct Voting

Delegation to Lawmakers is optional and always overridable. Every veCORTEX holder can:

- **Vote directly** on any proposal at any time during the voting period
- **Override** a Lawmaker's delegated vote by casting a direct vote (the direct vote replaces the delegated vote for that proposal)
- **Split delegation** across multiple Lawmakers for different vote weight portions
- **Revoke delegation** entirely and return to direct-only voting

Direct participation is the default state. Delegation is an opt-in convenience, not a requirement.
